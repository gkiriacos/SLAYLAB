Skills: Joins and Relates, Summarize Function, Calculate Feilds

Narrative: The population density per census tract map covers a larger area which can consist of several neighborhoods or communities. In addition to that the variability within the tract is a lot more smoothed out spatially, so smaller scale fluctuations are less apparent. With regards to ecological fallacy we should avoid assuming that households or people experience the same density because it could fluctuate more than it is apparent on the map. In terms of MAUP the choice of the tract boundaries used affects the patterns we could observe, and using a different tract could lead to different findings. The population density per block map shows a much more detailed and localized variation on a block by block basis. Using population density per block would reduce ecological fallacy because it would give a better insight to local dynamics than the tract map. With regard to MAUP the population density by block allows for a better analysis of smaller patterns.
